# Gerunds (İsmi fellər — "-ing" forması)

Gerund — felin "-ing" forması, amma cümlədə **isim** kimi işlənir.

---

## Gerund as Subject (Subyekt kimi)

**Swimming** is great exercise.
**Reading** every day improves your vocabulary.
**Working** from home has many advantages.

> Azərbaycanca: "Üzmək gözəl idmandır." — "Üzmək" isim kimi işlənir, eynilə.

---

## Gerund as Object (Obyekt kimi)

Bəzi fellər yalnız gerund qəbul edir:

### Verbs followed by Gerund only:

| Verb | Example |
|------|---------|
| **enjoy** | I enjoy **reading**. |
| **avoid** | She avoids **making** eye contact. |
| **finish** | He finished **writing** the report. |
| **mind** | Do you mind **waiting**? |
| **suggest** | She suggested **going** out. |
| **keep** | Keep **trying**! |
| **consider** | I'm considering **moving** abroad. |
| **admit** | He admitted **lying**. |
| **deny** | She denied **taking** the money. |
| **miss** | I miss **living** in Baku. |
| **risk** | Don't risk **losing** everything. |
| **can't help** | I can't help **laughing**. |
| **can't stand** | She can't stand **waiting**. |
| **give up** | He gave up **smoking**. |
| **put off** | Stop putting off **doing** your homework. |
| **carry on** | Carry on **reading**. |
| **practise** | Practise **speaking** every day. |
| **imagine** | Imagine **living** in Paris! |

---

## Gerund after Prepositions

Prepozisiyadan sonra həmişə gerund:

| Structure | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| interested **in** | She's interested **in learning** French. |
| good **at** | He's good **at playing** chess. |
| tired **of** | I'm tired **of waiting**. |
| worried **about** | She's worried **about failing**. |
| looking forward **to** | I'm looking forward **to seeing** you. |
| instead **of** | Call instead **of texting**. |
| before/after | **Before leaving**, check your phone. |
| without | She left without **saying** goodbye. |
| by | He improved by **practising** daily. |

> ⚠️ "Looking forward **to** + gerund" — "to" burada prepozisiyadır, infinitiv əlamətçisi deyil!
> ❌ I'm looking forward to **see** you.
> ✅ I'm looking forward to **seeing** you.

---

## Gerund after Certain Expressions

| Expression | Example |
|------------|---------|
| It's no use / no good | It's no use **arguing**. |
| It's (not) worth | It's worth **trying**. |
| There's no point in | There's no point **in waiting**. |
| Have difficulty/trouble | She has trouble **sleeping**. |
| Spend time | He spends hours **reading**. |
| Be used to | I'm used to **waking** up early. |
| Get used to | She's getting used to **driving** on the left. |

---

## Negative Gerund

**not** + gerund:

- She admitted **not knowing** the answer.
- I regret **not studying** harder.
- He's good at **not showing** his feelings.

---

## Possessive + Gerund (Formal)

| Informal | Formal |
|----------|--------|
| I don't mind him coming late. | I don't mind **his** coming late. |
| She was upset about me leaving. | She was upset about **my** leaving. |

---

## Common Mistakes

| Wrong | Correct |
|-------|---------|
| I enjoy to swim. | I enjoy **swimming**. |
| She avoided to make eye contact. | She avoided **making** eye contact. |
| He is good at to play chess. | He is good at **playing** chess. |
| I'm looking forward to see you. | I'm looking forward to **seeing** you. |
| She suggested to go out. | She suggested **going** out. |
