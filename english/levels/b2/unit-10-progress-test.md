# B2 Progress Test

## Bu Test Nəyi Yoxlayır?
B2 səviyyəsindəki bütün əsas mövzular:
- Advanced Grammar (Inversion, Cleft, Participle)
- Third Conditional & Mixed Conditional
- Passive Voice (bütün zamanlarda)
- Discourse Markers
- Academic Vocabulary
- IELTS Reading strategiyaları

## Bölmə 1: Qrammatika (30 bal)

### A. Third Conditional (5 bal)
1. He didn't study, so he failed. → If he ___ (study), he ___ (pass).
2. I didn't know about the meeting, so I didn't come. → If I ___ (know), I ___ (come).
3. They missed the deadline. They lost the contract. → If they ___ (not miss) it, they ___ (not lose) the contract.
4. She didn't apply early. She didn't get a place. → She ___ (get) a place if she ___ (apply) earlier.
5. We took the wrong road. We arrived late. → We ___ (not arrive) late if we ___ (take) the right road.

**Cavablar:**
1. had studied / would have passed
2. had known / would have come
3. had not missed / would not have lost
4. would have got / had applied
5. would not have arrived / had taken

### B. Mixed Conditional (3 bal)
1. I didn't study medicine. I'm not a doctor now. → If I ___ medicine, I ___ a doctor now.
2. She moved abroad. She isn't here now. → If she ___ abroad, she ___ here now.
3. He didn't save money. He can't buy a house now. → If he ___ money, he ___ able to buy a house now.

**Cavablar:** 1-had studied/would be, 2-hadn't moved/would be, 3-had saved/would be

### C. Passive Voice (5 bal)
1. They are building a new stadium. → A new stadium ___.
2. Someone had stolen the documents. → The documents ___.
3. They should upgrade the system. → The system ___.
4. They have delayed the announcement. → The announcement ___.
5. They were testing the new drug when the results arrived. → The new drug ___ when the results arrived.

**Cavablar:**
1. is being built
2. had been stolen
3. should be upgraded
4. has been delayed
5. was being tested

### D. Cleft Sentences (4 bal)
1. I need your support. → **What** I need ___.
2. John broke the window. → **It was John who** ___.
3. She wants honesty, not money. → **What** she wants ___.
4. The company failed because of poor management. → **It was poor management that** ___.

**Cavablar:**
1. What I need is your support.
2. It was John who broke the window.
3. What she wants is honesty, not money.
4. It was poor management that caused the company to fail.

### E. Inversion (3 bal)
1. I have never seen such a beautiful sunset. → **Never** ___
2. No sooner had we left than it started raining. ✓ (already correct)
3. Not only is the product expensive, it is also unreliable. ✓ (already correct)

**Cavab:** Never have I seen such a beautiful sunset.

### F. Discourse Markers (5 bal)
1. The plan is good. ___ (in addition), it is affordable.
2. ___ (despite) the challenges, the team succeeded.
3. He worked hard. ___ (consequently), he was promoted.
4. ___ (whereas) some prefer cities, others enjoy rural life.
5. ___ (admittedly), the study has limitations. ___ (nevertheless), the findings are significant.

**Cavablar:** 1-Furthermore/Moreover, 2-Despite, 3-Consequently, 4-Whereas/While, 5-Admittedly / Nevertheless

### G. Participle Clauses (5 bal)
1. Because she was exhausted, she went to bed. → ___ exhausted, she went to bed.
2. While he was running in the park, he saw an accident. → ___ in the park, he saw an accident.
3. The letter, which was written in 1850, was found in an attic. → ___ in 1850, the letter was found in an attic.

**Cavablar:** 1-Feeling/Being, 2-Running, 3-Written

## Bölmə 3: Oxuma (10 bal)

**Mətni oxuyun:**

> Artificial intelligence is transforming the way businesses operate. Companies that have adopted AI-driven tools report significant improvements in efficiency, with some claiming productivity gains of up to 40%. However, this rapid adoption has not been without controversy. Trade unions and workers' rights organisations argue that automation poses a serious threat to employment, particularly in sectors such as manufacturing, transportation, and customer service.
>
> Proponents of AI counter that historically, technological revolutions have ultimately created more jobs than they have destroyed. They point to the Industrial Revolution as evidence — despite initial fears of mass unemployment, the long-term effect was a dramatic expansion of the workforce. Nevertheless, critics note that the pace of AI development is unprecedented, leaving little time for workers and educational systems to adapt.
>
> Governments worldwide are grappling with how to regulate AI. Some have introduced legislation requiring companies to disclose when AI is used in hiring decisions. Others are investing in retraining programmes to help displaced workers develop new skills. What is clear is that the challenge requires a coordinated, international response.

**True / False / Not Given:**
1. All companies using AI have reported productivity gains of exactly 40%. → NOT GIVEN (some claim up to 40%)
2. The text says manufacturing workers are concerned about automation. → TRUE
3. The writer believes AI will definitely create more jobs than it destroys. → NOT GIVEN (proponents argue this, not the writer)
4. The Industrial Revolution resulted in long-term job growth. → TRUE
5. All governments have introduced the same AI regulations. → FALSE
