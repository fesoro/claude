# Saying No Politely — Nəzakətlə "Yox" Demək

## Səviyyə
B1 (iş yerində vacib!)

---

## Niyə Çətin?

"No" demək:
- Kobud səslənir
- Əlaqəni pozar
- Təhqir kimi qəbul edilə bilər

**Amma** "yes" həmişə demək:
- Çox iş yükü
- Burnout
- Peşəkarlıq aşağı düşür

**Həll:** Nəzakətli "yox" deməyi öyrən.

---

## Qızıl Qayda

"No" demək = 3 addım:

1. **Acknowledge** (tanı) — müraciəti qəbul et
2. **Explain** (izah et) — niyə yox
3. **Alternative** (alternativ) — başqa həll

---

## 1. Basic "No" Phrases

### Sadə yox

- "I'm sorry, I can't."
- "Unfortunately, I can't."
- "I won't be able to."
- "That's not possible for me."

### Professional

- "I don't think I can take that on."
- "I'm at capacity right now."
- "That falls outside my current priorities."

---

## 2. Acknowledging the Ask

Müraciəti qəbul et ki incimasın.

### Phrases

- "Thanks for thinking of me."
- "I appreciate you asking."
- "That sounds interesting."
- "I understand why you're asking."

### Example

- "**Thanks for thinking of me**, but I can't take this on right now."

---

## 3. Explaining (Brief!)

Qısa izah. **Detallar vermə.**

### ✓ Good reasons

- "I'm at capacity with current projects."
- "My plate is full right now."
- "I have other commitments."
- "I'm prioritizing X this week."

### ✗ Avoid (too personal)

- ~~"I'm too tired."~~
- ~~"I don't feel like it."~~
- ~~"My kid is sick."~~

---

## 4. Offering Alternative

"No" + solution = peşəkar.

### Alternativ tipləri

#### Başqasına yönəltmə

- "Have you asked X? They might have bandwidth."
- "I think Y would be a better fit for this."

#### Sonraya təxir

- "Can we revisit this next month?"
- "I could help in Q2, but not now."

#### Qismən kömək

- "I can't do X, but I can do Y."
- "I can't lead it, but I can support."

#### Resurs vermək

- "Here's a doc that might help."
- "Check out this tool / tutorial."

---

## 5. Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Ekstra iş

**Manager:** "Can you take on this new project?"

### ✓ Good response

"Thanks for the opportunity. **I'm at capacity** with the current
sprint. Could we **prioritize** what I should drop, or **delay**
this to next sprint?"

### ✗ Bad response

"Sure, I'll do it." (burnout)
"No." (kobud)

---

### Scenario 2: Meeting invite

**Colleague:** "Can you join this meeting?"

### ✓ Good

"Thanks for the invite. **My schedule is packed**. Can you
**share the notes** afterward? Happy to chime in **async**."

---

### Scenario 3: Help request

**Colleague:** "Can you help me debug this?"

### ✓ Good

"I'd love to help, but **I'm heads-down on** a critical fix.
Would **tomorrow morning** work? In the meantime, try checking
**[resource]**."

---

### Scenario 4: Weekend work

**Boss:** "Can you work this weekend?"

### ✓ Good

"I'll need to **check my family commitments** first. If it's
truly urgent, I can **make myself available**. If it can wait,
**Monday first thing** would work better."

---

### Scenario 5: Scope creep

**Client:** "Can you also add feature X?"

### ✓ Good

"That's a great idea. **It's outside the current scope**, but
let's **add it to the roadmap**. Happy to discuss in a follow-up."

---

## 6. Assertive "No" Templates

### Template 1

"**I appreciate** the ask, but **I can't commit to** [X] right now. **Here's why**: [brief reason]. **Could we** [alternative]?"

### Template 2

"**Unfortunately, I'm** unable to [X]. **My priorities this [period]** are [Y]. **Would it help if** [alternative]?"

### Template 3

"**I'm going to have to decline**. **I'm focused on** [priority]. **Someone who might help** is [person]."

---

## 7. Email "No"

### Template

```
Subject: Re: [Original Subject]

Hi [Name],

Thanks for reaching out about [topic].

Unfortunately, I'm not able to [X] because [brief reason].

However, [alternative/suggestion].

Please let me know if there's anything else I can help with.

Best,
[Your name]
```

### Example

```
Subject: Re: Can you review my PR?

Hi Alex,

Thanks for sending this over.

I'm swamped with the Q2 release this week, so I can't do
a thorough review right now.

Could you ask Sarah? She's worked on this area recently.

Happy to review any follow-up PRs.

Thanks,
Orkhan
```

## 8. Slack "No"

### Kısa

"Can't today, sorry! Tomorrow OK?"

### Polite

"Heads-down on X right now. Could we sync [time]?"

### With alternative

"I'm booked but @colleague might have time."

## 9. Saying No to Scope Creep

Tech kontekstində çox vacib!

### Phrases

- "That's a great idea, but it's **out of scope** for this sprint."
- "We can **add to the backlog** for future consideration."
- "Let's **ship the MVP first**, then iterate."
- "**Feature creep** will delay the release."

### Stakeholder management

- "I hear you. **Let's discuss trade-offs.**"
- "**If we add X, we'll need to drop Y.**"

---

## 10. Saying No to Boss

Çətin, amma vacib.

### ✓ Good approaches

- **Present alternatives**: "Here are three options..."
- **Show data**: "Current workload is X. This would push it to Y."
- **Ask for prioritization**: "What should I deprioritize?"

### Template

"I want to make sure I'm **focused on the right things**.
If I take on this, I'll need to **deprioritize X**. Is that
the **right trade-off**?"

---

## 11. Cultural Notes

### American

- Direct but polite
- "I can't" is acceptable
- Prefer clear "no"

### British

- More indirect
- "That would be tricky..."
- "I'm not sure I can..."

### Asian (Chinese, Japanese)

- Very indirect
- Rarely say outright "no"
- Use hints

### Azerbaijan context

- Balance: not too harsh, not too indirect
- **Professional directness** works in tech

---

## 12. Not to Do

### ✗ Avoid

#### Over-apologizing

- ~~"I'm so so sorry sorry please forgive me..."~~

#### Lying

- ~~"I have an emergency."~~ (if untrue)

#### Passive-aggressive

- ~~"Sure, whatever you want."~~ (don't say yes resentfully)

#### Vague

- ~~"Maybe. I'll see."~~ (false hope)

---

## 13. Pure "No" Phrases (Strong!)

Bəzən birbaşa "no" lazım.

### Strong but professional

- "That won't work for me."
- "I'm going to have to decline."
- "I'm not comfortable with that."
- "This isn't something I can do."

### When to use

- Harassment / inappropriate asks
- Ethical issues
- Clear boundaries

---

## 14. "Soft No" Phrases

Pozitiv vs negativ yumuşatma.

### Soft softeners

- "I wish I could, but..."
- "Unfortunately..."
- "Sadly, I'm unable to..."
- "I'd love to, but..."

### But avoid endless softening

Balance ilə: yumuşaq + aydın.

---

## 15. Follow-up

"No" dedikdən sonra:

### If relationship important

- "Let me know if there's another way I can help."
- "Happy to chat about it later."

### Keep door open

- "Ask me again in a month."
- "Let's revisit this."

## Interview Nümunələri

### "Tell me about saying no"

Prepare a story:

1. **Situation**: context
2. **Why you had to say no**
3. **How you communicated**
4. **Outcome** (positive reframe)

### Example

"My manager asked me to take on a side project during a
critical release. I said: **'I want to support this, but
I'm focused on the Q2 release. Can we revisit after
shipping?'** She agreed, and I delivered both well."

## Azərbaycanlı Səhvləri

- ✗ "No, I can't." (kobud, abrupt)
- ✓ "**Unfortunately, I can't** because [brief reason]."

- ✗ "I don't want to." (təmkinsiz)
- ✓ "**This doesn't fit** my current priorities."

- ✗ Uzun-uzadı izah
- ✓ **Qısa** (bir cümlə) reason kifayətdir.

---

## Xatırlatma

**"No" Formulu:**
1. ✓ **Acknowledge** ("Thanks for asking")
2. ✓ **Explain** ("I'm at capacity")
3. ✓ **Alternative** ("Could we revisit?" / "Try @X")

**Qızıl qayda:**
- Clear "no" > unclear "maybe"
- Brief reason > long excuse
- Offer alternative > flat rejection

**Top phrases:**
- "I'm at capacity."
- "My plate is full."
- "It's outside my scope."
- "Could we revisit?"
- "Have you tried asking X?"
