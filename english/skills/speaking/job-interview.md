# Job Interview English

---

## 1. Common Interview Questions & Model Answers

### "Tell me about yourself."
> "I have a background in [field] and have been working in [area] for [X] years. In my most recent role, I was responsible for [task]. I'm now looking for an opportunity to [goal]."

### "Why do you want this job?"
> "I'm particularly drawn to this role because [reason]. I believe my experience in [area] would allow me to [contribution]. I'm also impressed by [company's value/project]."

### "What are your strengths?"
> "One of my key strengths is [strength]. For example, in my previous role, I [specific example]. I also tend to [second strength], which helped me [result]."

### "What is your weakness?"
> "I used to struggle with [weakness], but I've been working on this by [action taken]. As a result, I've [improvement]."

### "Where do you see yourself in five years?"
> "I'd like to develop my skills in [area] and take on more [responsibility/leadership]. I hope to be contributing at a senior level within this organisation."

### "Why are you leaving your current job?"
> "I've learned a great deal in my current role, but I feel I'm ready for a new challenge. I'm looking for a position where I can [growth area]."

## 2. Useful Phrases

### Asking for clarification
- "Could you clarify what you mean by...?"
- "Do you mean in terms of...?"
- "I just want to make sure I understand the question correctly."

### Buying time to think
- "That's an interesting question. Let me think for a moment."
- "I'd like to consider that briefly before answering."

### Describing experience
- "In my previous role, I was responsible for..."
- "I have extensive experience in..."
- "I've had the opportunity to work on..."
- "One project I'm particularly proud of is..."

### Talking about skills
- "I'm highly proficient in..."
- "I have a strong background in..."
- "I work well under pressure / in a team / independently."

### Asking questions at the end
- "What does a typical day in this role look like?"
- "What are the main challenges someone in this position would face?"
- "How would you describe the team culture here?"
- "What are the opportunities for professional development?"

## 4. STAR Technique (for competency questions)

Use STAR to structure answers to "Tell me about a time when..." questions.

| Letter | Meaning | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **S** — Situation | Set the context | "In my previous job, we had a tight deadline..." |
| **T** — Task | Describe your responsibility | "I was responsible for coordinating the team..." |
| **A** — Action | Explain what you did | "I organised daily check-ins and prioritised tasks..." |
| **R** — Result | State the outcome | "We delivered the project two days early." |

---

## 5. Things to Avoid

- Criticising your previous employer
- Giving vague answers ("I'm a hard worker" without examples)
- Saying you have no weaknesses
- Answering too briefly or too long (aim for 1–2 minutes per answer)
- Using very informal language or filler words ("like", "you know", "um")
