# Sentence Building Exercise (Cümlə Qurma Məşqi)

---

## Exercise 1 — Word Order

Bu sözləri düzgün sıra ilə düzəlt:

1. often / she / to / goes / gym / the
2. been / have / I / never / Paris / to
3. yesterday / finished / I / my / homework / early
4. interesting / was / film / that / very / an
5. hardly / he / ever / late / is

---

## Exercise 2 — Sentence Combining

İki sadə cümləni bir cümləyə birləşdir (verilmiş sözlə):

1. She was tired. She continued working. **(although)**
2. He studied hard. He passed the exam. **(so)**
3. I woke up late. I missed the bus. **(because)**
4. She loves coffee. She also loves tea. **(not only... but also)**
5. We arrived early. The shop was closed. **(however)**
6. He saves money. He wants to travel. **(in order to)**
7. She called him. He didn't answer. **(but)**
8. The weather was bad. We went hiking. **(despite)**

---

## Exercise 3 — Cleft Sentences

Vurğulanan hissəni cleft sentence ilə ifadə et:

Nümunə: "John broke the window." (vurğu: John)
→ "It was John who broke the window."

1. "Mary wrote the report." (vurğu: Mary)
2. "The meeting was cancelled because of the storm." (vurğu: storm)
3. "I need some rest." (What... structure)
4. "She passed because she worked hard." (reason)
5. "They met in Paris." (vurğu: Paris)

---

## Exercise 4 — Inversion

Bu cümlələri inversion ilə yenidən yaz:

1. I have never heard such beautiful music.
2. She not only lost her keys but also her wallet.
3. He realized his mistake only after the meeting.
4. I had barely sat down when the phone rang.
5. You can succeed only if you work hard.

---

## Exercise 5 — Paragraph Writing

Bu bullet points-dən bir abzas yaz (PEEL quruluşu ilə):

**Topic:** Technology affects communication.
- People can connect instantly
- Distance doesn't matter anymore
- Example: video calls, messaging apps
- Result: relationships are maintained more easily

---

## Answers (Cavablar)

### Exercise 1:
1. She often goes to the gym.
2. I have never been to Paris.
3. I finished my homework early yesterday.
4. That was a very interesting film.
5. He is hardly ever late.

### Exercise 2:
1. Although she was tired, she continued working.
2. He studied hard, so he passed the exam.
3. I missed the bus because I woke up late.
4. Not only does she love coffee, but she also loves tea.
5. We arrived early; however, the shop was closed.
6. He saves money in order to travel.
7. She called him, but he didn't answer.
8. Despite the bad weather, we went hiking.

### Exercise 3:
1. It was Mary who wrote the report.
2. It was the storm that caused the meeting to be cancelled.
3. What I need is some rest.
4. The reason she passed is that she worked hard.
5. It was in Paris that they met.

### Exercise 4:
1. Never have I heard such beautiful music.
2. Not only did she lose her keys, but she also lost her wallet.
3. Only after the meeting did he realize his mistake.
4. Hardly had I sat down when the phone rang.
5. Only if you work hard can you succeed.

### Exercise 5 (Sample):
> Technology has fundamentally transformed the way people communicate. This is because modern devices allow people to connect instantly regardless of geographical distance. For example, tools such as video calling apps and instant messaging platforms enable people to maintain close relationships with friends and family on the other side of the world. As a result, physical distance has become far less of a barrier to meaningful communication than it once was.
