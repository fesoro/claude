# B1 Reading Comprehension — B1 Səviyyə Oxu Məşqləri

## Bu Fayl Haqqında

5 orta uzunluqlu mətn (200-300 söz) + anlama və detallı sualları + true/false + açıqlama sualları. B1 mətnləri daha abstraktdır — fikir, səbəb, müqayisə bildirir.

**Qayda:** Sözləri bilmədikdə lüğətə qaçma. Kontekst və cümlə strukturundan məna çıxarmağa çalış.

## Text 1: Remote Work — Benefits and Challenges

Since 2020, millions of people around the world have started working from home. At first, it was because of the pandemic, but many companies have decided to continue with remote work permanently. The question now is: is remote work better or worse than working in an office?

There are clear advantages. People save time because they don't need to travel to work. They can live anywhere — even in small towns — and still work for big companies. Many workers say they are more productive at home because there are fewer distractions.

However, remote work also has problems. Some people feel lonely because they don't see their colleagues. It can be difficult to separate work from personal life — you might work longer hours without noticing. Young employees often struggle because they miss the chance to learn from more experienced coworkers in the office.

The best solution, according to experts, is hybrid work. Employees come to the office two or three days a week and work from home the rest of the time. This way, they get the benefits of both options without the main disadvantages.

Companies are still experimenting to find what works best. What seems clear is that fully going back to the old office-only system is unlikely.

### Questions
1. When did remote work become common?
2. What are three advantages of remote work mentioned in the text?
3. Why can remote work be hard for young employees?
4. What is hybrid work?
5. What do experts think about the future of office-only work?

### True / False
6. All companies stopped remote work after the pandemic.
7. Remote workers are always more productive than office workers.
8. Hybrid work combines office and remote days.

### Discussion (open-ended)
9. Would you prefer remote, office, or hybrid work? Why?

### Answers
1. Since 2020 (because of the pandemic).
2. Save time (no commuting), can live anywhere, fewer distractions / more productive.
3. They miss the chance to learn from experienced coworkers in the office.
4. Employees work some days at the office and some days from home (2-3 days office, rest at home).
5. They think it's unlikely to return fully to office-only.
6. **False** — "many companies have decided to continue with remote work permanently."
7. **False** — the text says "many workers **say** they are more productive," not "always."
8. **True**.

### Key Vocabulary
- pandemic = pandemiya
- permanently = daimi olaraq
- distractions = diqqəti yayındıran şeylər
- separate work from personal life = işi şəxsi həyatdan ayırmaq
- experienced coworker = təcrübəli iş yoldaşı
- hybrid = qarışıq, hibrid

## Text 2: The Interview That Changed Everything

When Arif applied for his current job two years ago, he was very nervous. It was his first interview for a senior position, and the company was one of the biggest tech firms in Europe. He prepared for three weeks — he practiced answers, studied the company, and even recorded himself speaking English.

On the day of the interview, everything that could go wrong, went wrong. His taxi was late because of heavy traffic. His laptop battery died right before the meeting. And when the interview started, his video connection was terrible.

But Arif didn't panic. He apologized calmly, switched to audio only, and focused on giving clear answers. When the interviewer asked a difficult technical question, he didn't pretend to know. He said, "I'm not sure about the exact solution, but I would approach it by..." and explained his thinking.

Two days later, he got the job. During his first week, his new manager told him something surprising. "We liked your technical skills," she said, "but what really impressed us was how you handled the problems. That's what we wanted to see."

Now, when Arif gives advice to younger developers preparing for interviews, he says: "Don't try to be perfect. Show how you react when things go wrong. That's real life."

### Questions
1. Why was Arif nervous before the interview?
2. How long did he prepare?
3. List three things that went wrong on the interview day.
4. How did Arif react when he didn't know the answer to a technical question?
5. What does Arif's manager say impressed them the most?
6. What advice does Arif give now?

### Analysis
7. In your own words, explain the main lesson of this story (2-3 sentences).

### Answers
1. It was his first interview for a senior position at a big company.
2. Three weeks.
3. Taxi was late (heavy traffic), laptop battery died, video connection was terrible.
4. He admitted he wasn't sure, then explained his thinking process.
5. How he handled problems (not just his technical skills).
6. Don't try to be perfect; show how you react when things go wrong.
7. (Sample answer) Interview success doesn't require a perfect performance. How you react under pressure — staying calm, being honest, showing your thinking process — matters more than having all the right answers.

### Key Vocabulary
- senior position = yüksək vəzifə
- heavy traffic = sıx tıxac
- pretend = özünü (nəyisə) kimi göstərmək
- approach (a problem) = yanaşmaq
- handle (problems) = idarə etmək, öhdəsindən gəlmək

## Text 3: Why We Forget What We Learn

Have you ever studied for an exam, felt confident, and then forgotten everything a week later? You're not alone. Scientists call this "the forgetting curve," and it affects everyone.

According to research, we forget about 50% of new information within the first hour. After 24 hours, we remember only 25%. After a week, less than 10% stays in our memory — unless we review the material.

The good news is that there is a simple solution: spaced repetition. Instead of studying for a long time once, review a little bit many times. For example, if you learn 20 new English words today, review them tomorrow, then in three days, then in a week, then in a month. Each time, your brain pushes the information deeper into long-term memory.

This technique is not new. Students, musicians, and athletes have used it for centuries. What's new is that apps like Anki and Quizlet now use smart algorithms to tell you exactly when to review each piece of information.

Another important factor is context. We remember things better when we connect them to real situations. Learning the word "deadline" in a sentence about your job is more effective than memorizing it from a list.

So, if you are studying English — or anything else — don't try to learn everything at once. Review often, use context, and trust the process. Your brain will thank you later.

### Questions
1. What is "the forgetting curve"?
2. How much do we forget within one hour?
3. What is spaced repetition?
4. What is the difference between spaced repetition and regular studying?
5. Why is context important for memory?
6. What is the writer's advice at the end?

### Answers
1. The fact that we forget a lot of what we learn quickly over time.
2. About 50%.
3. Reviewing information in small amounts, many times, with increasing gaps between reviews.
4. Regular studying = long session once; spaced repetition = short sessions multiple times (tomorrow, in 3 days, week, month).
5. Because we remember information better when we connect it to real situations.
6. Review often, use context, trust the process — don't try to learn everything at once.

### Key Vocabulary
- review = təkrar etmək, yoxlamaq
- long-term memory = uzunmüddətli yaddaş
- spaced repetition = aralıqlı təkrar
- algorithm = alqoritm
- context = kontekst
- effective = təsirli

## Text 4: The Coffee Problem

Ayten works as a product manager at a startup. She drinks six cups of coffee every day — two in the morning, two at lunch, and two in the afternoon. For years, she thought this was normal. Everyone at her office drinks a lot of coffee.

Last month, Ayten started having trouble sleeping. She would go to bed at 11 pm, but couldn't fall asleep until 2 or 3 am. She felt tired all day. Her work started to suffer — she made small mistakes in meetings and forgot important tasks.

She went to see a doctor. After asking several questions, the doctor said, "How much coffee do you drink?" When Ayten told him, he shook his head. "That's your problem. Caffeine stays in your body for up to 10 hours. If you drink coffee at 3 pm, it's still in your system at midnight."

The doctor gave her a plan: reduce coffee slowly, not suddenly. For the first week, drink five cups. Next week, four. Continue until you reach two cups, only in the morning.

At first, Ayten had headaches and felt even more tired. But after three weeks, everything changed. She started sleeping through the night. She had more energy. She felt calmer. She was surprised — she didn't realize coffee was the cause of so many problems.

Now, Ayten tells her colleagues: "Drinking a lot of coffee doesn't make you more productive. It just makes you feel like you are."

### Questions
1. What is Ayten's job?
2. How many cups of coffee did she drink before?
3. What problems did coffee cause her?
4. Why shouldn't she drink coffee at 3 pm according to the doctor?
5. How did the doctor tell her to reduce coffee?
6. What happened in the first week of reducing coffee?
7. After three weeks, how did Ayten feel?
8. What is the message Ayten shares with her colleagues?

### Answers
1. Product manager at a startup.
2. Six cups a day.
3. Trouble sleeping, feeling tired all day, making mistakes at work, forgetting tasks.
4. Because caffeine stays in the body for up to 10 hours — still in her system at midnight.
5. Slowly, not suddenly — reduce by one cup each week.
6. She had headaches and felt even more tired.
7. Slept better, had more energy, felt calmer.
8. Drinking a lot of coffee doesn't make you productive; it just makes you feel like you are.

### Key Vocabulary
- startup = yeni-qurulan şirkət
- have trouble sleeping = yatmaqda çətinlik çəkmək
- fall asleep = yuxuya getmək
- suffer = əziyyət çəkmək, pisləşmək
- reduce = azaltmaq
- suddenly = qəflətən
- sleep through the night = gecə boyu yatmaq

## Text 5: Why Some Meetings Don't Work

Meetings are supposed to help teams work better. But often, they do the opposite. A recent study found that employees spend 31 hours a month in unproductive meetings — that's almost a full working week wasted.

What makes a meeting bad? First, there is often no clear agenda. People join without knowing what will be discussed. Second, too many people are invited. If a meeting has 10 people, only 2-3 usually speak. The rest just listen, often while checking email on their laptops. Third, meetings go longer than planned. A "30-minute sync" often becomes a 50-minute discussion.

Good meetings share a few things in common. They have a clear goal — "Decide the launch date" is better than "Discuss the launch." They include only the people who really need to be there. And they start and finish on time.

Some companies have started using simple rules. Amazon, for example, bans slideshows in meetings. Instead, the meeting starts with everyone reading a written document silently for 15 minutes. Then the discussion begins — but it's based on facts, not opinions.

Other companies have "no-meeting days." On these days, employees can focus on deep work without interruptions. Many developers say these are the days they get the most done.

So, if you feel your team has too many meetings, you're probably right. The solution is not to cancel all meetings, but to make sure each meeting has a purpose — and to leave space for real work.

### Questions
1. How many hours a month do employees waste in meetings?
2. List three reasons meetings fail, according to the article.
3. Why is "Decide the launch date" better than "Discuss the launch"?
4. What is Amazon's rule about meetings?
5. What is a "no-meeting day"?
6. What is the writer's final advice?

### True / False
7. Having more people in a meeting makes it more productive.
8. Amazon meetings start with reading a document.
9. "No-meeting days" help developers focus.

### Discussion
10. Describe one bad meeting you've been in. What went wrong?

### Answers
1. 31 hours (almost a full working week).
2. No clear agenda, too many people invited, meetings go longer than planned.
3. Because it's a clear goal (action/decision), not a vague topic.
4. They ban slideshows; everyone reads a written document silently first, then discusses.
5. A day when no meetings are scheduled, so employees can focus on deep work.
6. Don't cancel all meetings, but ensure each has a purpose and leaves space for real work.
7. **False** — only 2-3 out of 10 usually speak.
8. **True**.
9. **True**.

### Key Vocabulary
- unproductive = səmərəsiz
- agenda = gündəlik, görüş proqramı
- launch = buraxılış, işə salma
- ban = qadağan etmək
- slideshow = slayd-şou
- interruption = kəsinti, yarımçıq qoyma
- cancel = ləğv etmək
- purpose = məqsəd

## Ümumi Strategiyalar (B1)

### 1. Əvvəl baxış (Preview)
- Başlığı oxu
- İlk abzası oxu
- Sualları oxu
- Sonra mətni oxu

### 2. Açar sözləri tap
- Hər abzasda ən vacib 1-2 söz
- Bu sözlər abzasın mövzusunu göstərir

### 3. Konteksdən təxmin et
- Bilmədiyin sözü daşımaq → abzasdakı digər sözlərə bax
- Məsələn, "he felt exhausted after the long run" — exhausted = yorğun

### 4. Abstrakt fikirlər üçün
- "Yazar nə demək istəyir?" sualını ver
- Konkret nümunə + ümumi fikir həmişə birlikdədir
