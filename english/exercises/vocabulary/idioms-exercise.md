---

## Exercise 1 — Match the Idiom to Its Meaning

| Idiom | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1. break the ice | a. to make a decision |
| 2. bite the bullet | b. to start a conversation in a social situation |
| 3. burn the midnight oil | c. very rare |
| 4. once in a blue moon | d. to work very late |
| 5. hit the nail on the head | e. to endure pain or difficulty |
| 6. spill the beans | f. to say exactly the right thing |
| 7. under the weather | g. to reveal a secret |
| 8. piece of cake | h. feeling ill |
| 9. cost an arm and a leg | i. very expensive |
| 10. cut corners | j. something very easy |
| 11. go back to square one | k. to do something poorly to save time/money |
| 12. jump on the bandwagon | l. to start again from the beginning |
| 13. let the cat out of the bag | m. to reveal a secret accidentally |
| 14. see eye to eye | n. to follow a popular trend |
| 15. the ball is in your court | o. to agree with someone |

2. After months of difficulty, we're finally starting to __.
3. Before we proceed, let's make sure everyone is __.
4. The problem is complex — we need to __.
5. Don't __ — you already have three projects.

## Exercise 3 — Correct Usage

Bu idiomlar doğru işlənibmi? Əgər yanlışdırsa, düzəlt:

1. "The exam was a piece of cake — it was very difficult."
2. "She felt under the weather, so she stayed home from work."
3. "He burned the midnight oil to finish the report on time."
4. "The project cost an arm and a leg — it was very cheap."
5. "They see eye to eye on everything — they always argue."
