## Exercise 7 — Mixed — Choose the Correct Word

1. Everyone __ (accept/except) John came to the meeting.
2. I need to __ (lose/loose) some weight before summer.
3. She is __ (than/then) taller than her sister.
4. The __ (principal/principle) of the school gave a speech.
5. This tie __ (complements/compliments) your shirt perfectly.
6. He gave her a __ (complement/compliment) on her presentation.
7. The __ (economic/economical) situation in the country is improving.
8. This car is very __ (economic/economical) — it uses little fuel.
9. That was a __ (historic/historical) moment for the country.
10. She's researching __ (historic/historical) documents from the 18th century.

## Answers

### Exercise 1:
1. affect (verb)
2. effect (noun)
3. affect (verb)
4. effect (noun)
5. affects (verb)

### Exercise 2:
1. They're
2. there
3. Their
4. there
5. their

### Exercise 3:
1. It's (it has)
2. its (possessive)
3. It's (it is)
4. its (possessive)
5. It's (it is)

### Exercise 4:
1. lie (intransitive)
2. laid (transitive past: lay→laid)
3. lay (past of lie)
4. lay (transitive)
5. lying (present participle of lie)

### Exercise 5:
1. rises (intransitive)
2. raise (transitive)
3. rose (intransitive past)
4. raise (transitive)
5. rises (intransitive)

### Exercise 6:
1. advice (noun)
2. advise (verb)
3. advice (noun)
4. advised (verb past)
5. advice (noun)

### Exercise 7:
1. except
2. lose
3. than
4. principal (school head)
5. complements (goes well with)
6. compliment (praise)
7. economic (related to economy)
8. economical (uses little fuel = efficient)
9. historic (important moment)
10. historical (related to history)

### Exercise 8:
1. **affected** (verb needed)
2. **They're** (they are)
3. **its** (no apostrophe for possessive)
4. She **laid** the book (transitive verb)
5. Prices have **risen** (rise → risen)
6. He **advised** me (verb form)
7. Everyone **except** the manager
8. These trousers are too **loose** (adjective)
