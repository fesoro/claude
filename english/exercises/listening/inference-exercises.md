# Inference Exercises — B1

İnference — deyilməyəni başa düşmək. Natiq birbaşa demir — sən tondan, kontekstdən, sözlərin seçimindən mənasını çıxarmalısan.

Bu bacarıq danışıqda, interview-da, remote işdə kritikdir. Tez-tez insanlar nə düşündüklərini açıq demir — xüsusilə iş mühitində.

---

## Nümunə (How it works)

**Dialog:**
> **A:** I finished the feature.
> **B:** ...Right. Let me take a look before we push it.

**Sual:** What does B really mean?
- A. He is happy with the work
- B. He wants to check the quality first *(implied: he has doubts)* ✓
- C. He will push it immediately

**İzahat:** *"...Right."* — fauzla başlayan, qısa cavab. Razı olan insan *"Great!"* və ya *"Nice one!"* deyər. *"Let me take a look"* — "mən özüm baxım" — birbaşa etiraz etmədən şübhəni bildirir.

## Exercise 1 — Manager Feedback

**Scenario:** Manager, developer-in son həftəki işi haqqında danışır.

**Dialog:**
> **Manager:** So, I reviewed what you submitted last week.
> **Developer:** How did it look?
> **Manager:** It works. The core logic is there. I think with a bit more time, you could really polish it up.
> **Developer:** Oh, should I make changes now?
> **Manager:** No, no — it's fine for now. We'll come back to it in the next sprint.

**Suallar:**
1. What does the manager really think about the developer's work?
2. Why does the manager say *"it's fine for now"* instead of *"it's great"*?
3. What does *"with a bit more time, you could really polish it up"* imply?

**Cavablar + İzahatlar:**

1. **The manager thinks the work is acceptable but not good enough.** — *"It works"* — minimum bar. Həvəsli rəhbər *"I loved it"* və ya *"really well done"* deyər. *"Core logic is there"* — digər hissələr var ki, orada deyil.

2. **It signals low-key disappointment without conflict.** — *"Fine for now"* — "bu an üçün keçər" — gizli tənqid. İngilis mədəniyyətində *"fine"* tez-tez "yaxşı" mənasına gəlmir — "keçər" mənasındadır.

3. **It implies the current quality needs improvement.** — *"could really polish it up"* — "cilalamaq lazımdır" — polish = hazırda hamar deyil deməkdir. *"Could"* — bacarıq yox, nəzakətli tövsiyə.

## Exercise 2 — Colleague Saying "I'm Fine"

**Scenario:** Qısa video call-dan əvvəl iki developer danışır.

**Dialog:**
> **Alex:** Hey, how are you doing? You look a bit tired.
> **Maria:** I'm fine. Just a long week.
> **Alex:** Did something happen with the release?
> **Maria:** It's... being handled. Don't worry about it.
> **Alex:** Okay. Let me know if you need anything.
> **Maria:** Yeah. Thanks.

**Suallar:**
1. Is Maria actually fine? What signals tell you this?
2. What does *"it's being handled"* suggest?
3. Why does Maria say *"Don't worry about it"* to Alex?

**Cavablar + İzahatlar:**

1. **No, she is not fine.** — *"Just a long week"* — problemi minimuma endirmək cəhdi. *"It's being handled"* — narahat olduğunu göstərir. Normal insan *"Yeah, everything's good!"* deyər.

2. **It suggests there is a problem and she is managing it, possibly alone.** — *"Being handled"* — passive voice — kim həll edir? Demir. Tez-tez özü həll etmək məcburiyyətindədir, kömək istəmir və ya istəyə bilmir.

3. **She doesn't want to share details or appear weak.** — İş mühitində insanlar problemi paylaşmaqdan çəkinir, xüsusilə həll edilmədisə. *"Don't worry"* — "mövzunu bağlayıram" siqnalıdır.

## Exercise 3 — Code Review Disagreement

**Scenario:** Senior developer, pull request-ə rəy verir. Junior developer cavab verir.

**Dialog:**
> **Senior (Emma):** I left a comment on the caching logic. Have you seen it?
> **Junior (Liam):** Yeah, I saw it. I actually thought about that approach too, but I went a different direction.
> **Emma:** Right. Can you walk me through your reasoning?
> **Liam:** Sure. I figured this way would be simpler for the next developer who reads it.
> **Emma:** Interesting. I think there might be a performance trade-off we should consider.
> **Liam:** Oh — is it blocking the merge?
> **Emma:** Not blocking, no. But let's revisit it together.

**Suallar:**
1. Does Emma agree with Liam's approach?
2. What does *"interesting"* mean in this context?
3. Is Emma satisfied with *"simpler for the next developer"* as a reason?

**Cavablar + İzahatlar:**

1. **No, she disagrees but is being polite.** — *"Can you walk me through your reasoning?"* — "məntiqini izah et" — birbaşa rədd etmədən sual verir. Razı olan insan *"Ah, got it, makes sense"* deyər.

2. **"Interesting" = polite skepticism.** — İngilis danışıq mədəniyyətində *"interesting"* tez-tez "razı deyiləm amma açıq deyirəm" mənasındadır. *"That's great!"* deyilmirsə — diqqətli olun.

3. **No — she immediately counters with a performance point.** — *"I think there might be a performance trade-off"* — *"simpler"* arqumentini qəbul etmir. *"Might be"* — nəzakətli forma, amma narahatlığı var.

## Exercise 4 — HR Salary Negotiation

**Scenario:** Developer interview sonrası HR ilə danışır.

**Dialog:**
> **Developer:** I was hoping we could discuss the salary a bit. The offer is slightly below what I had in mind.
> **HR:** I completely understand. We do work within a defined band for this role.
> **Developer:** Is there any flexibility there?
> **HR:** We've tried to put together a package that reflects your experience. The base is set, but there are other components — performance bonuses, equipment allowance — that add up.
> **Developer:** I see. So the base salary itself is not negotiable?
> **HR:** What I can say is that we're already at the top of the band for this level.

**Suallar:**
1. What is HR really telling the developer about the salary?
2. Why does HR mention bonuses and allowances?
3. What does *"we're already at the top of the band"* mean in practice?

**Cavablar + İzahatlar:**

1. **The salary is not negotiable.** — *"We work within a defined band"* + *"the base is set"* + *"top of the band"* — üç dəfə eyni mesaj. Heç biri birbaşa "no" demir amma bütün yolları bağlayır.

2. **To make the total package look better without changing the base salary.** — Developer base salary istəyir, HR bonus/allowance-a yönləndirir. Bu klassik "redirect" texnikasıdır — birbaşa soruya birbaşa cavab vermirlər.

3. **It means: we cannot go higher, take it or leave it.** — *"Already at the top"* — "artıq maksimumda" deməkdir. "Bu leveldən yuxarı getmək üçün başqa level lazımdır" da bildirilə bilər.

## Exercise 5 — Team Lead Implying Urgency

**Scenario:** Team lead, standup-da bir task haqqında danışır.

**Dialog:**
> **Team Lead:** So where are we with the payment gateway integration?
> **Developer:** I'm about halfway through. Should be done by end of week.
> **Team Lead:** Okay. Just so you know — the sales team has a demo with a big client on Thursday.
> **Developer:** Oh. Is that... related to this?
> **Team Lead:** They'll be showing the checkout flow.

**Suallar:**
1. What is the team lead actually communicating?
2. Why doesn't the team lead say "you need to finish it by Thursday"?
3. How should the developer respond to understand this correctly?

**Cavablar + İzahatlar:**

1. **The task must be finished before Thursday, not end of week.** — Team lead birbaşa demir, amma *"demo on Thursday"* + *"showing the checkout flow"* = "Cümə axşamına qədər hazır olmalıdır" mesajı.

2. **Avoiding direct pressure — leaving it to the developer to "figure out".** — Bəzi rəhbərlər birbaşa əmr vermək əvəzinə "kontekst verərək" developer-in özünün çıxarış etməsini gözləyir. Bu üsul iş yerinin mədəniyyətindən asılıdır.

3. **Say: "Got it — I'll make sure it's ready before Thursday."** — Developer siqnalı anladığını göstərməlidir. Susmaq = anlamadım kimi görünür.

## Exercise 6 — Question That Is Actually a Complaint

**Scenario:** Colleague, başqa bir developer-ə Slack-da yazır.

**Dialog:**
> **Mia:** Hey, quick question — are we still supposed to push directly to main?
> **Tom:** Yeah, for now we are. Why?
> **Mia:** Oh, nothing. I just saw a few commits come in this morning that broke the build. I wasn't sure if I missed a policy change or something.
> **Tom:** Ah. No, it's still the same.
> **Mia:** Got it. Thanks.

**Suallar:**
1. Is Mia really asking about the policy?
2. What is her actual message to Tom?
3. What does *"I wasn't sure if I missed a policy change"* imply about others?

**Cavablar + İzahatlar:**

1. **No — she already knows the policy.** — "Are we still supposed to push directly to main?" — bu sual deyil, giriş. Cavabı bilir.

2. **She is complaining that someone broke the build by pushing to main carelessly.** — *"I saw commits that broke the build"* — bu əsl mesajdır. Birbaşa ittiham etmək əvəzinə sual kimi çatdırır.

3. **It implies: someone else pushed without following rules (or maybe Tom himself).** — *"I wasn't sure if I missed something"* — özünü günahlandırır gibi görünür, amma əslində *"başqası nəsə etdi"* deyir. Bu "passive blame" texnikasıdır.

## Exercise 7 — Client "Suggesting" a Requirement

**Scenario:** Client ilə video call. Developer, completed feature göstərir.

**Dialog:**
> **Client:** Looks good! I really like the overall layout.
> **Developer:** Thanks, glad you like it.
> **Client:** One small thing — I was thinking, it might be nice if users could also filter by date range. Not a must-have, but it would be a useful little addition.
> **Developer:** Sure, I can look into it.
> **Client:** Great. Actually, I was going to mention it to your PM as well — just so everyone's aligned.

**Suallar:**
1. Is the date range filter really "not a must-have"?
2. What does *"I was going to mention it to your PM"* signal?
3. How should the developer respond to protect scope?

**Cavablar + İzahatlar:**

1. **No — it's a requirement, not a suggestion.** — *"Not a must-have"* — client özü belə deyir, amma davranışı əks onu göstərir. PM-ə demək niyyəti = ciddi istəkdir.

2. **It's a soft escalation — she will make it official if the developer doesn't commit.** — *"Just so everyone's aligned"* — "PM-i loop-a salacam" deməkdir. Bu, birbaşa "əmr" deyil amma əsas subyektlərə çatdıracaq.

3. **Say: "Before we commit to it, let me check with the PM on scope and timeline."** — Developer nə "yes" nə "no" deməlidir — mövzunu PM-ə yönləndirməlidir. *"I can look into it"* həddindən artıq açıq sözdür, client bunu "yes" kimi qəbul edir.

## Exercise 8 — Deadline Concern Without Saying It

**Scenario:** Sprint review hazırlığı zamanı iki developer danışır.

**Dialog:**
> **Dev 1 (Sam):** How's the API integration going?
> **Dev 2 (Priya):** Yeah, it's moving. There were a few more edge cases than I expected.
> **Sam:** Cool. Demo is Friday, so...
> **Priya:** Yeah, I know. I'm working through it.
> **Sam:** Do you want me to pick up the error handling part? I've got some capacity.
> **Priya:** I mean... if you want to. I wasn't going to ask, but it would help.

**Suallar:**
1. What is Priya's real situation with the task?
2. Why doesn't Priya directly say "I won't finish in time"?
3. What does *"I wasn't going to ask, but it would help"* reveal?

**Cavablar + İzahatlar:**

1. **She is behind schedule and concerned she won't finish before Friday.** — *"More edge cases than expected"* — iş düşünüldüyündən çoxdur. *"I'm working through it"* — "çalışıram amma bitirmirəm" mənasındadır.

2. **She doesn't want to admit she's struggling — fear of looking incompetent.** — İş mühitində "bitirə bilmirəm" demək çətin görünür, xüsusilə gənc developer-lər üçün. *"I wasn't going to ask"* — özündən kömək istəməmişdi.

3. **She IS struggling but cannot bring herself to ask for help directly.** — *"I wasn't going to ask"* = "kömək lazımdır amma deməkdən çəkinirdim." *"It would help"* = "çox lazımdır." Bu indirect help request-dir.

## Inference İpuçları — Cheat Sheet

| Söz/İfadə | Tez-tez Real Mənası |
|---|---|
| *"It's fine."* | Yaxşı deyil, amma mübahisə etmirəm |
| *"Interesting."* | Razı deyiləm / şübhəliyəm |
| *"Not a must-have."* | Əslində istəyirəm, amma açıq deməkdən çəkinirəm |
| *"Can you walk me through it?"* | Razı deyiləm, izah et |
| *"Just so you know..."* | Bunun üzərindən düşün — dolayı nəzakətli əmrdir |
| *"I wasn't going to ask, but..."* | Əslində köməyə ehtiyacım var |
| *"We work within a defined band."* | Maaş müzakirəsi mümkün deyil |
| *"That's one way to look at it."* | Yanlışdır amma birbaşa demir |
| *"It would be nice if..."* | Bunu istəyirəm / gözləyirəm |
| *"I'll mention it to [someone]."* | Rəsmi edəcəm — nəzərə al |
