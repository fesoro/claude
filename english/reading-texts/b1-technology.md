# B1 Reading: How Smartphones Have Changed Our Lives

---

## Text

It is hard to imagine life without a smartphone today. These small, powerful devices have completely transformed the way we communicate, work, and entertain ourselves. But are smartphones always good for us?

**Communication**

The most obvious change is in how we communicate. Before smartphones, people wrote letters, sent emails from computers, or made calls from landlines. Now, we can contact anyone in the world within seconds. Messaging apps like WhatsApp allow people to send text, voice messages, photos, and videos for free. Video calls make it possible to see family members who live thousands of kilometres away.

**Work and Productivity**

Smartphones have also changed the workplace. Many people can now work from anywhere — a café, a park, or even on public transport. This flexibility has been positive for many workers. However, it also means that people are often "always on" — they receive work emails and messages even at weekends and during holidays. Some studies suggest that this constant connectivity is causing increased stress and burnout.

**Entertainment and Social Media**

People now spend an average of four to five hours per day on their smartphones, much of it on social media. Apps like Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube provide endless entertainment. However, research shows that spending too much time on social media can lead to feelings of loneliness, anxiety, and low self-esteem, particularly in young people.

**Conclusion**

Smartphones have brought many benefits to modern life. They keep us connected, informed, and entertained. However, it is important to use them in a balanced way. Being aware of how much time we spend on our phones — and what we use them for — can help us get the benefits without the negative effects.
